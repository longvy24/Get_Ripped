The Four Rules That Don't Break

  1. Hit protein every day. Miss carbs, miss fat, miss workouts before you miss protein.
  2. Train five days a week, every week. If life detonates, you do three. Not zero.
  3. Weigh in once a week — same day, same time, after bathroom, before food.
  4. If you slip, the next meal is your reset. Not next Monday.

Cardio & Mobility

Cardio Zones

Zone 1 (Z1) — Active Recovery

Full conversation possible. HR 50–60% max. Long walks, easy bike. Burns calories without taxing recovery. Use on rest days and Saturdays.

Zone 2 (Z2) — Steady State

Conversational but you'd rather not. HR 65–75% max. Gold standard for fat loss. Treadmill at 3.5 mph / 8% grade is perfect.

HIIT — High Intensity Intervals

Hard efforts followed by recovery. Used sparingly. Common formats: 1 min on / 1 min off × 8 rounds, or 30 sec on / 30 sec off × 12 rounds. Bike, rower, AirBike preferred.

Sprints

25–30 sec near-max effort, 90 sec full recovery, 6–10 rounds. Reserve for Saturday in Phase 3.

      🌾 Sunday breakfast prep — Overnight Oats Jars (5 minutes for the week):
      1. Grab 5 mason jars or glass containers with bamboo lids
      2. In each: 1/2 cup rolled oats + 1 cup whole milk + 1 scoop whey + 1 tbsp PB + cinnamon. Stir.
      3. Slice 5 bananas, drop one into each jar (or add fresh banana each morning if you prefer firmer fruit)
      4. Seal, fridge. Eats best 12-72 hours later — soaked oats are creamy.
      5. Grab one each morning. Eat with a spoon in the car or at your desk. No cooking, no thinking.

      Wed (legs day) and Sun (rest day) get a different version — adjust portions in those jars accordingly. The Power Shake alternative is a 60-second blend if you want hot variety or forgot to prep.

      🍳 Recipe Variations — Primary Protein Dishes

      Your go-to recipes for lunch and dinner protein. Each is macro-fit for your meal slots, with specific swaps from the original recipes to land in your daily targets. Use these in the meal rotation slots — same base proteins, different cuisines, zero boredom.

      The weekly meal-prep approach: pick ONE recipe per week. Cook a giant batch on Sunday that covers all 14 main meals (lunch + dinner × 7 days). Scale each recipe ~3-4× its base size. One cook day, one cleanup, one ingredient list. Rotate to a different recipe next week so you don't get bored.

      Sunday Cook Plan — Weekly Batch Sizes

      Each recipe scaled up to ~14 servings for the full week. Cook time ~75-90 min total for the protein + sauce. Rice gets cooked separately in a big pot (~5 cups dry → ~15 cups cooked).

      🍳 One-Pot Spaghetti & Meat Sauce Week

      • Recipe base = 4 servings. Scale 3.5× for the week.
      • Shopping: ~3.5 lb ground beef 90/10, 14 oz Italian sausage, 2 onions, 14 cloves garlic, 4 oz tomato paste, 2.5 tbsp BTB, 2 × 28 oz crushed tomatoes, ~42 oz dried pasta, 4 oz Parmesan
      • Cook in your biggest Dutch oven or stock pot. Two batches if needed.
      • Reheats beautifully — actually better day 3-4 as flavors deepen.

      🍳 Pad Kra Pao Week

      • Recipe base = 3 servings. Scale 5× for the week.
      • Shopping: ~5 lb 90/10 ground beef, 5 tbsp each fish sauce/oyster sauce/soy sauce, 10 tsp dark soy, ~5 tsp sugar, 2.5 cups broth, 2.5 tbsp oil, 10 shallots, 30 cloves garlic, ~15 Thai chilies, ~5 cups Thai basil, 14 dozen eggs (1 per meal — fry fresh)
      • Pre-prep aromatics (chop shallots/garlic) the night before. Stir-fry in 2 batches.
      • Basil added at the END of each batch only — wilts the same day.

      🍳 Dakgangjeong Week

      • Recipe base = 4 servings. Scale 3.5× for the week.
      • Shopping: ~5 lb boneless skinless chicken thighs, 4 tbsp potato starch, 4 tbsp oil, sauce: ~1.5 cups ketchup, 7 tbsp brown sugar, 4 tbsp ACV, 4 tbsp soy sauce, 20 cloves garlic, 4 tsp sesame oil, 8 tbsp gochujang, sesame seeds + scallions
      • Pan-fry chicken in 4-5 batches (don't crowd pan), keep crispy pieces warm in oven 200°F. Toss all in big bowl with sauce at the end.
      • Will lose some crispness in the fridge — still delicious as saucy chicken.

      🍳 Soy Glazed Chicken Week

      • Recipe base = 4 servings. Scale 3.5× for the week.
      • Shopping: ~5 lb boneless skinless chicken thighs, 3.5 tbsp garlic minced, ~10 tbsp soy sauce, ~12 tbsp oyster sauce, ~1.5 tbsp dark soy, 3.5 tsp oil, 3-4 cans Coke Zero (~42 fl oz total)
      • Marinate chicken Sunday morning, pan-fry in 4-5 batches afternoon. Combine all in big pot with Coke Zero, reduce to a thick glaze.
      • Reheats fine — glaze gets stickier on day 2-3.

      Portion plan per day: Lunch = 1 serving + 1 cup rice. Dinner = 1.25-1.5 servings + 1.5 cups rice (post-WO macro target). Weekend portions match weekdays except Sat (no training day = same as lunch) and Sun (rest day, lower rice). Total weekly: ~13-14 servings of protein + 15 cups of rice cooked. Buy your containers ahead of time — 14 glass containers + a big rice container, all in the fridge by Sunday night.

      Budget Strategy — Eat Big, Spend Less

      Groceries got expensive. This plan still works on a tight budget — you just have to be deliberate about where and what you buy. Follow these eight rules and you'll cut your weekly bill by 30–40% without dropping a single gram of protein.

      1. Bulk-buy protein at warehouse clubs

      Costco, Sam's Club, BJ's. Bone-in chicken thighs for $1.50/lb (vs $4-6 retail for breast), 5-lb tubes of 80/20 ground beef, 18-egg flats. Membership pays for itself in 3 trips. Don't have one? Walmart, Aldi, or Food Lion bulk packs are next best.

      2. Eggs are the cheapest complete protein

      Roughly $0.20–0.30 per egg = $1.50 per 30g of complete protein. Nothing else comes close. Make eggs your default breakfast protein. 5 eggs is 30g protein for under $2.

      3. Trade up to cheaper cuts

      • Chicken thighs (bone-in, skin-on) — half the price of breast, more flavor, basically the same protein per oz
      • Ground beef 80/20 — $1-2/lb less than 90/10, drain the grease
      • Pork loin chops or pork shoulder — $2-3/lb (vs tenderloin at $7)
      • Replace steak with ground beef seasoned well — fraction of the cost, same macros

      4. Store brand on everything

      Cheese, yogurt, pasta, bread, milk, butter, marinara, salsa, peanut butter, oats — the store-brand version is made in the same factory as the name brand 80% of the time. Saves 20-30% across the cart with zero downside.

      5. Buy Parmesan in a block, shred fresh

      Cheese is out of the regular rotation — but you still need a small Parmesan block for pasta. Pre-grated Parmesan in the green can is ~3× the price per oz and tastes like cardboard. Buy a 4-6 oz block, store in the fridge, grate fresh onto pasta. Lasts ~3 weeks.

      6. Carbs are basically free

      White rice, oats, potatoes, pasta — when you buy these in bulk (10-20 lb rice bag, 10 lb potato bag) you pay under $1 per pound. Skip bagels, sweet potatoes, fancy bread, granola — these are 3-5x the price of plain alternatives.

      7. Sunday meal prep, not daily cooking

      Cook 5 lb of chicken thighs in the oven at once. Cook a giant pot of rice. Bake a tray of potatoes. Brown 3 lb of ground beef. Portion into containers. Drives the per-meal cost down and removes the "what should I eat" decision that leads to takeout.

      8. Cheap whey is fine whey

      Skip the $80 fancy isolate. Buy NOW Foods, Body Fortress, MyProtein, or store-brand whey concentrate in 5-lb tubs. About $40-55. 5 lbs lasts ~5 weeks of daily shakes. Same amino acid profile.

      What to Drop From the Original Plan (Too Expensive Right Now)

      • Bacon — $7-9/lb. Replace with extra eggs. Almost double the protein per dollar.
      • Sirloin steak — $10-14/lb. Replace with ground beef 80/20 ($4/lb) seasoned well.
      • Pork tenderloin — $7/lb. Replace with pork loin chops or shoulder ($2-3/lb).
      • Branded deli turkey & ham — $10-14/lb. Replace with leftover oven-cooked chicken thighs sliced cold.
      • Frozen mixed berries — $6-8/lb. Replace with frozen strawberries only ($3/lb) or sliced banana.
      • Almonds & walnuts — $10-15/lb. Replace with peanut butter (much cheaper protein per dollar).
      • Bagels, cream cheese, Cheerios, granola — drop entirely. Same macros from oats, white bread, rice.
      • Premium whey isolate — drop. Cheap whey concentrate is fine.

      ✅ Keep — switch to cheaper brand

      • Multivitamin — Drop branded fitness multis. Use Equate (Walmart), Kirkland (Costco), or Nature Made. $5-10/bottle for 100+ days.
      • Whey protein — Drop MuscleTech. Use NOW Foods, Body Fortress, MyProtein, or Kirkland Signature from Costco. ~$40-55 for 5 lbs = 5-6 weeks of shakes.

      • Fish oil (Nature Made) — This is the smartest item in your stack. You're not eating fish, so this is your only omega-3 source. Nature Made at Walmart is solid quality at a fair price. Don't downgrade further.

      ⏬ Reduce frequency

      • Gorilla Mode Pre-Workout — Strong product, no argument. Problem is daily use. Limit to 2-3 sessions per week — leg day Wednesday + one or two heavy upper days. Black coffee + a banana 30 min before lifting works fine the other days. Saves $15-20/month and protects your sleep + recovery.

Combined with your deficit, hard training, and a separate stim pre-workout, you're set up for elevated heart rate, sleep problems, and a hard crash in week 8 when the plan needs you sharpest.
      • OxyShred — Same category, double the cost, same problem. Never stack two thermogenics — it's wasted money and quadruple the side-effect risk.

      The Hard Truth on Fat Burners

      Fat loss happens from a calorie deficit. No pill or powder changes that. Every fat burner on earth is some combination of caffeine, green tea extract, L-carnitine, yohimbine, and herbal stimulants. The marginal calorie burn is real but tiny — well under 100 calories a day, far less than you'd think. The side effects (sleep disruption, elevated resting heart rate, anxiety, crashing energy mid-cut) are the bigger problem. Your training plan and food plan are designed to drop body fat on their own. Stack stimulants on top and you'll feel great for 3 weeks and terrible for the rest of phase 2 and 3.
